canned mackerel stew

an easy, surprisingly delicious way to fit seafood into your weekly intake

ingredients

  • 2 cans mackerel
  • 2 medium onions (chopped)
  • 1 to 2 cups celery (chopped)
  • 32 oz. stock or broth (chicken or veg—or use 1/2 stock 1/2 water)
  • 1 can tomato paste
  • 1-2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 Tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 Tbsp PARSLEY (chopped)
  • seasonings of choice (I use black pepper, smoked paprika, and turmeric)
  • Optional: red pepper (cajun seasoning, to taste) dried jalapenos (to taste)

instructions

  1. Drain mackerel, break fish apart, and set aside in a bowl.
  2. Heat avocado oil in a pot and sauté onions and celery for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the tomato paste and stock. A full can of tomato paste can be used, but adjust the amount to your liking. Use two cans of stock (32 oz) or just use 1 can stock and 1 can water.
  4. Bring pot to a boil and cook for 2 minutes, stirring as needed.
  5. Add fish and return pot to boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. While simmering, add lemon juice and seasonings. Stir occasionally and add water if needed. Serve over rice or with crackers or bread, or all on its lonesome.
